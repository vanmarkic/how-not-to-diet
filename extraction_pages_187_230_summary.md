## NEW Foods Extracted (6 items)

Starting from food-188 as requested:

### 1. **Millet** (food-188)
- **Pages**: 219, 246, 250-252, 254-255
- **Key Info**: Generic term for many small grains (pearl, proso, foxtail, finger millet)
- **Benefits**: Contains resistant starch that feeds gut bacteria; markedly slower stomach-emptying time than white rice, potatoes or pasta
- **Notable Quote**: "Millet is a generic term applicable to many small grains. Much of its starch may be resistant starch that feeds the good bugs in our colon. Millet also tends to have markedly slower stomach-emptying times than other starchy foods." (p255)
- **Categories**: intact-whole-grains, high-fiber, low-glycemic, resistant-starch

### 2. **Fenugreek (Ground)** (food-189)
- **Pages**: 169-170, 222-223, 337
- **Key Info**: Traditional spice in Ethiopian and Indian cuisine
- **Benefits**: Anti-inflammatory properties; slightly bitter, maple-like flavor
- **Usage**: 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon per recipe (4 servings)
- **Categories**: herbs-and-spices, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant-rich

### 3. **Nutmeg (Ground)** (food-190)
- **Pages**: 222-223, 294-295
- **Key Info**: Warm, sweet, aromatic spice
- **Benefits**: Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties; pairs with fruits and breakfast foods
- **Usage**: 1/8 to 1/4 teaspoon per recipe (4 servings)
- **Categories**: herbs-and-spices, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant-rich

### 4. **Sage** (food-191)
- **Pages**: 121, 178-179, 202, 208
- **Key Info**: Available fresh or dried/ground; strong, earthy, slightly peppery flavor
- **Benefits**: Powerful anti-inflammatory herb with potential cognitive benefits
- **Usage**: 1/2 teaspoon fresh chopped or dried/ground per recipe
- **Categories**: herbs-and-spices, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant-rich

### 5. **Malt Vinegar** (food-192)
- **Pages**: 208, 230
- **Key Info**: Made from malted barley; darker, maltier flavor than other vinegars
- **Benefits**: Helps spices adhere to vegetables; adds complex tang
- **Usage**: 1/3 cup for coating vegetables, or spritz bottle for roasting
- **Categories**: vinegar, low-calorie, flavor-enhancer

### 6. **Cornmeal / Polenta** (food-193)
- **Pages**: 208, 216-217
- **Key Info**: Coarse-ground corn; blue cornmeal has additional antioxidants
- **Benefits**: Whole grain fiber; blue variety offers anthocyanin antioxidants
- **Usage**: 1/3 to 3/4 cup for coating/crusting (4 servings)
- **Categories**: whole-grains, high-fiber, gluten-free
