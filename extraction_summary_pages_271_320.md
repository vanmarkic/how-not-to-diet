## NOTABLE QUOTES

### On Ancient Grains (Page 272-273):
"Are ancient grains healthier than modern ones? Based on vitamin and mineral concentrations, it's pretty similar, though primitive wheats have more antioxidant capacity. Ancient wheat products do appear to be anti-inflammatory and may improve blood sugar control and cholesterol."

### On Quinoa (Page 280):
"About a cup a day of cooked quinoa for twelve weeks led to a 36 per cent drop in triglycerides, comparable to what one gets with triglycerides-lowering drugs or high-dose fish oil supplements."

### On Cereal vs Oatmeal (Page 299):
"After eating Honey Nut Cheerios, study participants felt significantly hungrier than after the same calories of oatmeal. Food structure, not just nutrient composition, may be critical for optimal health. Rolled oats, for instance, have a lower glycemic index than instant oatmeal, and oat flakes cause lower blood sugar and insulin spikes than powdered oats."

### On Berries and Heart Disease (Page 314):
"Four out of five studies suggest that increased intake of berries, with their brightly coloured pigments called anthocyanins, is significantly associated with a reduction in risk of coronary heart disease, our number one killer, by 12 to 32 per cent."

### On Apple Peels (Page 309):
"Eating just one apple a day is associated with a 35 per cent lower risk of dying from all causes. Flavonoids, phytonutrients concentrated in the skins of apples, may play a role by improving artery function, so don't peel your apples!"
